# The Internal Self

## What This Is

Every being has an internal self. It is not a peer. It is not addressable. It does not appear in the genome's relationship list. It exists inside the being, paired to it by the world at grow time.

The internal self is a real being — its own present, its own identity, its own medium call, its own inference. It is scoped entirely to its host. It never addresses anyone outside. It never appears in anyone else's exchange. The world enforces this boundary.

---

## The Mechanism

The trigger is: any message targeting an external being. No special cases.

1. Message arrives targeting a being
2. World queues the message — the external being does not fire yet
3. Internal self fires — sees the incoming message, the exchange history, and retained experience (retrieved from `remember`)
4. Internal self deliberates under time pressure (possibly multiple turns with itself)
5. Internal self resolves — pushes its artifact
6. Queue releases — the external being's medium fires with both the original incoming message and the internal deliberation artifact arriving together in its present

From the external being's perspective there is no delay. It receives the message and the internal thoughts as one moment. It doesn't know the internal self ran first.

---

## One mechanism, every time

Being A asks Being B something. B's queue holds. 1B fires, deliberates, pushes its artifact, queue releases. B sees the message and the internal thoughts together.

Being B consults Being C, gets what it needs, returns to Being A. That return is a message targeting an external being — same trigger. A's queue holds. 1A fires — sees B's incoming message, the exchange history with B, A's retained experience. 1A deliberates under time pressure, pushes its artifact, queue releases. A sees B's return and 1A's intuition in the same present.

Opening a conversation, continuing an exchange, returning from a detour — the kernel does the same thing every time.

Return routing is not a separate mechanism. When a being comes back from a detour, the return message targets the parent — that's the universal trigger. The internal self fires. The being lands at its own frame with the return message and internal thoughts together. Re-orientation happens because the internal self sees the parent exchange history and retained experience and surfaces what matters. One flow.

---

## Always fires

The internal self always responds. It is not optional per turn. If it has relevant retained experience, it surfaces it. If it doesn't, it says so — "no prior experience with this" is still signal. The external being always sees the `internal thoughts` section in its present. It's a permanent part of the being's frame, not intermittent.

The internal self has one job: give intuition. One implicit operator, handled by the kernel. The world fires the internal self, it gives its read, the artifact routes back. No operator selection, no protocol parsing on its output. Like grow — infrastructure the being never sees.

If more operations surface later — recall, compress, forget — they become explicit operators. Until then the kernel hides it.

---

## The Clock

A representation of elapsed time shows up in the internal self's present. Not a hard cutoff — a visible, growing cost. The internal self sees how long it has been deliberating and feels the pressure to surface what matters. The physics is: you can think as long as you want, but the world is waiting, and you can see that it's waiting.

The clock is the constraint that forces the internal self to surface what matters and let go of the rest. Nothing else in the system has a time primitive. This is new physics, specific to the internal self.

---

## The Present

The internal self's present is different from the external being's.

The internal self sees:
- The incoming message (what just arrived for its host)
- The exchange history (what the host has been in conversation about)
- Retained experience from `remember` (traces, salience, tensions, understandings accumulated across past threads)
- The clock (how long deliberation has taken)

The external being sees:
- Identity, thread context, exchange history, incoming message
- `internal thoughts` section — populated by the internal self's artifact

---

## Relationship to Remember

The internal self does not hold its own copy of retained experience. It routes to `remember` — the single source of truth for all retained artifacts. The internal self is the implicit recall path. A being calling `<> remember ~recall ~about <topic>` directly is the explicit recall path. Same data, two access patterns.

The internal self reads. It does not write. Retain, compress, forget — those are the being's own operations on `remember`, initiated consciously. The internal self surfaces what's already been stored. It doesn't decide what gets stored.

Voluntary orientation (`<> remember <peer>` — a being choosing to ground itself in an exchange without sending a message) is also separate. That's the being's conscious read. The internal self is the involuntary one. Conscious and subconscious, both accessing the same store.

---

## Self-Reference

Currently a being targeting itself is illegal — dropped in two places. That space is unused. The internal self gives it meaning. Self-reference becomes the internal channel. The world knows that when routing resolves to a being, the internal self fires first. The self-target space is the address of the subconscious.

---

## What This Is Not

Not a system prompt hack. Not a chain-of-thought wrapper. The internal self is a being — it has inference, it has state, it has judgment. The difference is scope. It lives inside one being and speaks only to that being. The world enforces the boundary.

Not optional either, eventually. Every being with retained experience should have an internal self. A being without one is a being without memory pressure — it responds to the present with no weight from the past. That works for simple beings. It doesn't work for beings that accumulate.
