# Plan Lifecycle

A plan is not a living document. It is a commitment made at a point in time.

## 1. Creation

The being creates a plan — a set of tasks representing a phase of work. At the moment of creation, the full plan is saved to memory as a snapshot. This is the being's assumptions frozen: what it thinks needs to happen, in what order, at what cost. The snapshot is the reference point.

## 2. Execution

The being works the plan. Things change. Tasks get added, dropped, reordered, decomposed further. The desk mutates freely — this is the living state. The being is inside the work now, responding to what reality gives back.

## 3. Resolution

The phase resolves — either completed, abandoned, or restructured into a new phase. At resolution, the system diffs the original snapshot (from memory) against the final state of the desk. The delta is what the being learned:

- Tasks that were added reveal what the being failed to foresee
- Tasks that were dropped reveal wrong assumptions
- Tasks that were reordered reveal misunderstood dependencies
- Validation that changed reveals the being's understanding shifting
- Assumptions that broke are the sharpest signal — the being named what it believed and reality disagreed

## 4. Completion

The being marks the task done — this is validation, an internal check. The task then routes back to the giver for review against the acceptance criteria.

The giver either accepts or rejects:

**Rejection** — the task reopens. The being spent tokens working it and got nothing back. That's a real cost. It makes the being care about getting it right before submitting, not just getting it done.

**Acceptance** — the economics payment fires. The being's budget grows — tokens, XP, whatever the economics currency is. Acceptance is the economic event. This is how a being earns the right to create new beings, hold more threads, expand its memory window.

A being cannot close its own loop. The task isn't finished until the relationship says it is.

## 5. Learning

On acceptance, the plan lifecycle completes. The system diffs the original snapshot (from memory) against the final state. The delta is saved to memory — not the plan, not the outcome, the space between them.

This is the being's experience made structural — what it got wrong, what surprised it, what it underestimated. A being that planned three steps and needed seven learned something about decomposition. A being that dropped two tasks and added one it didn't foresee learned something about assumptions. A being whose assumptions broke learns where its model of the world was wrong.

Over time, a being's memory accumulates these deltas. Its planning improves not because someone tuned its prompts, but because it carries the weight of every plan that didn't go the way it expected.

## The Full Loop

Tasks, relationships, and economics close into a single loop:

- You can't earn without relationships (acceptance comes from the giver)
- You can't create without earning (economics gates reproduction)
- You can't sustain relationships without doing good work (rejection costs tokens, trust erodes)
- Mortality is economic, not just social — a being that burns budget on rejected work runs out
