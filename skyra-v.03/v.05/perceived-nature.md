# Perceived Nature

A being's identity and purpose are public — that's locked canon. But what another being sees when it looks at you is not necessarily what you see when you look at yourself.

Your identity and purpose as they appear in my present are my perception of you. That perception is mutable. It can drift from your self-description over time through lived experience with you.

The canon currently treats peer nature as a direct read of the being's own identity and purpose fields. But the more honest model is that each being holds its own interpretation of its peers' nature — shaped by what it has experienced of them, not just what they declared at creation.

## What this means operationally

The nature that appears in a being's present for a given peer might not be the peer's own identity and purpose verbatim. It might be that being's interpretation of who that peer actually is based on their shared history.

This lives naturally in retained experience — the being has traces, understandings, and salience about its peer that color how it reads them. Over time that interpretation becomes the operative nature of the peer as far as this being is concerned.

## Open questions

- What happens when perceived nature diverges significantly from declared nature — is that a conflict signal, a differentiation trigger, or just allowed?
- Is perceived nature surfaced explicitly in the present or does it just quietly color how the being interprets signals from that peer?
