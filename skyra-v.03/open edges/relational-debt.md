# Relational Debt

An open exchange is an obligation.

When a signal arrives from a peer and opens an exchange, the receiving being owes that peer something back. The debt is local — it lives on the relationship between those two beings. The being doesn't need to know why it was called or what's happening upstream. It just knows the exchange is open and something is owed.

When the being closes the exchange, the debt is paid.

This chains naturally. Strategy owes prefrontal. Prefrontal owes whoever called it. Each being resolves its own local debt. The full response arc emerges from that without any being needing to carry the whole stack.

The external conversation is still the anchor — it's what gives the chain its orientation — but no being below the boundary needs to know that.

## One obligation per exchange

An exchange opens once something is needed and closes once that thing is resolved. One open obligation per exchange per relationship at a time.

## Debt visibility is exchange-scoped

A being may have multiple open obligations across different exchanges — some from one external thread, some from another. The present only surfaces the obligations relevant to the exchange currently active. Debts belonging to a different external thread are not visible.

This means debt visibility is filtered by exchange context, not just by peer. The present shows who you owe and who owes you within the current thread only. Obligations from other threads are real but out of scope until that thread becomes active.

The exchange ID tracing back to the external conversation is what makes this filtering possible.

## Self-relationships may be special

When a being goes internal, it's working — not requesting. The self-exchange may be the workspace where obligations to peers get resolved, not a place where new obligations accumulate.

Tentative rule: obligations only exist on peer exchanges, not self exchanges. The self is where you do the work that satisfies them.

## Open questions

- How does the obligation surface in the present? Does the being see "exchange open with prefrontal" explicitly, or does it infer from the exchange state?
- What happens if a being closes a peer exchange without having satisfied it — is that a drop, a conflict signal, or just allowed?
- Can a being have multiple open exchanges with different peers simultaneously, each carrying its own obligation?
- Self-exchange obligations: if a being calls itself expecting a different reasoning pass to return something, does that open an obligation? Or is the self always just workspace?
