# Callable Language Seeding

The genome seeds relationships but not callable language. Peers appear in the present with identity and purpose but no language. Inference is guessing the protocol from scratch on every turn.

Beings know who their peers are but not how to speak to them. That's why exchanges feel loose — the cognitive network is there structurally but not usable in practice.

What needs to happen:

- Internal genome relationships (prefrontal→strategy, prefrontal→values, etc.) need callable language seeded in genome.skyra via ~language-<peer>
- That language gives inference the syntax to actually use the relationship
- External and novel relationships earn callable language through exchange — that's the emergent path

The present already renders callable language if it exists on the channel. The infrastructure is there. The genome just isn't populating it.

Also: callable language needs to be enforced at the kernel level. If a being fires at a peer and the expression doesn't match the callable language on that channel, the kernel rejects it. This might be a bitch to implement.
