# External Exchange In Present

Each external thing — email, michael talking, a game, a call — gets its own I/O pair. An inbound being and an outbound being. Their exchange is the external conversation. That pair owns it.

When a signal moves inward, the external exchange travels with it. Not a summary, not a relay hop. The actual exchange. Each internal being inherits the external exchange from the sender as context in its present. The external exchange doesn't mutate as it moves — it's the ground truth every internal being is oriented toward.

Nobody writes to it except the I/O pair that owns it.

By the time motor fires, it's still that exchange. The one that started with michael. Or the email. Unbroken chain of custody.

Multiple external things can be live simultaneously. Each has its own I/O pair, its own exchange, its own thread. They don't interfere.

Solves Issue 5 and Issue 6 at the same time — external origin never dissolves.
