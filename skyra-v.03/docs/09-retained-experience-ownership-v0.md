# Retained Experience Ownership v0

## Purpose

This document locks ownership and access rules for retained experience in the
current `v.03` model.

## Ownership

Beings own their own retained experience.

Retained experience is the cumulative mark of what a being has lived through
relating.

It does not live in a shared global store.

## The Personal Memory Pair

Every being is created with:

- its own personal hippocampus
- its own personal experience store being

Those companion beings are instantiated on the being's creation path.

The hippocampus is the librarian.

It writes retained experience to the right store and reads it back when the
being thinks.

The experience store holds the retained experience.

Neither companion being owns it.

The being that lived it does.

## Local By Default

Retained experience is local by default.

A being cannot directly ask another being's hippocampus or experience store for
that being's retained experience.

That would bypass the relationship.

If another being's experience is relevant, the legitimate path is to relate to
that being.

## Privacy Is Structural

This is not a bolt-on security rule.

It is part of the architecture.

## Short Framing

Retained experience belongs to the being that lived it.

The personal hippocampus indexes it.

If you want another being's experience, you have to relate to that being.
