# Experience Layer (Prelim)

## Core Framing

Experience is the retained pattern that emerges from past interactions as interpreted through cognition.

It is selective, persistent, and used to inform future cognition.

Contract-level experience draws from this layer.

The contract determines what portion of the experience layer a node is permitted to operate over.

## What Experience Contains

Think in terms of what kinds of patterns can exist, not how they are stored.

### A. Entity Formation

"this thing exists"

Examples:

- a person
- a project
- a tool
- a recurring topic

Experience allows the system to recognize something again.

### B. Relationships

"these things are connected"

Examples:

- Mike <-> Northeastern
- Project <-> deadline
- Capability <-> API

This is where structure starts forming.

### C. Patterns Over Time

"this tends to happen"

Examples:

- user studies at night
- infra upgrades cause downstream issues
- certain requests lead to follow-ups

This is temporal intelligence.

### D. Preferences / Tendencies

"this is usually favored or avoided"

Examples:

- prefers structured outputs
- avoids over-engineering
- likes short iterative docs

This is mostly Life-side, but System can have analogs such as preferred routes or stable flows.

### E. Salience / Importance Signals

"this matters more"

Examples:

- school > side project in a given period
- prod issues > dev cleanup
- certain people or goals carry weight

This is critical for selection and attention.

### F. Capability Awareness (System-Side)

"this can be done"

Examples:

- this API exists
- this node can handle X
- this route is valid

This is how System builds its world model.

### G. Constraints / Boundaries

"this cannot or should not happen"

Examples:

- auth limitations
- contract boundaries
- safety or correctness constraints

This is important for both Life and System.

## What Experience Does

Experience is useful because of how it is used.

### A. Supports Recall

Allows cognition to pull relevant patterns into the current frame.

### B. Shapes Interpretation

The same stimulus is interpreted differently depending on experience.

### C. Guides Decision Making

Influences what gets selected, prioritized, or ignored.

### D. Enables Continuity Without Explicit Linking

Even without `intent_id`, things can feel connected.

### E. Reduces Ambiguity

Fills in gaps when input is underspecified.

## Life vs System

Experience appears to exist in at least two domains.

### Life-Oriented Experience

- entities: people, projects, goals, routines
- relationships: personal, temporal, contextual
- patterns: preferences, habits, importance

### System-Oriented Experience

- entities: capabilities, tools, interfaces
- relationships: dependencies, routes, permissions
- patterns: execution flows, failure modes, optimal paths

## What Experience Is Not

Experience is not:

- a full history log
- every interaction
- raw cognition
- a single global object
- guaranteed to be consistent or complete

It is also not identical to contract-level experience.

Contract-level experience is a bounded access surface over this layer.

## Open Edges

The following remain open:

- how does cognition write to experience?
- what determines retention, such as salience, repetition, intent, or something else?
- how is experience retrieved into frame?
- do Life and System share storage or just abstraction?

## Short Framing Block

Experience is the retained pattern that emerges from past interactions as interpreted through cognition.

It is selective and persistent, and informs future interpretation, recall, and decision-making.

Experience may include:

- entities and their relationships
- patterns over time
- preferences and tendencies
- salience and importance signals
- capabilities and constraints

Experience is not a full record of history, but a structured retention of what continues to matter.

It appears to exist in at least two domains:

- Life-oriented experience, or user world
- System-oriented experience, or capabilities and execution

The mechanisms for retention, retrieval, and update remain open questions.
