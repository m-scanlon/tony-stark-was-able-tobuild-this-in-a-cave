# second level primitves of understanding 

perceive — to notice or detect something entering the system’s awareness
synthesize — combine perceived or parsed elements into a coherent structure the system can reason about
interpret — assign meaning to the structured input within the system’s current perception frame
analyze — examine the interpreted information to identify relationships, patterns, or implications
internalize — incorporate the analyzed meaning into the system’s internal model, updating how future stimuli are perceived and understood


# refelct - Creates wisdom - not always available not in v1
examine the system’s current perception frame or reasoning state to identify gaps, inconsistencies, or new questions generating new stimulus for further understanding.


# perceive - decides what enters perception - not in v1

detect - the system senses that a singal exists
focus - the system decides to pay attention to it

This model assumes the system passively receives stimuli for now. In future iterations, additional primitives (e.g., active sensing or probing the environment) may be introduced when the system begins intentionally exploring or querying its environment.

# synthesize - operates on perception - not in v1

group — combine related elements in the perception frame into provisional units inside the perseption frame 
relate — establish relationships between grouped elements inside the perception frame 
structure — form a coherent representation that can be interpreted

# interpret - assign meaning to the same perception frame

reference — connect the structured representation to relevant concepts, prior knowledge, or history in the system’s model
infer — derive likely meanings, implications, or candidate explanations from the current frame using those references

resolve — select and refine the most coherent meaning for the current perception frame
validate — test whether the resolved meaning is supported by the current frame and does not conflict with available evidence


# internalize - Not in v1

evaluate — determine whether the current understanding is important or reliable enough to update the model
integrate — incorporate the new knowledge into the system’s model
stabilize — update the model so future perception and interpretation use this new understanding v2


# analyze not in v1

probe — inspect the interpreted frame for deeper structure, uncertainty, or consequence
trace — follow implications, dependencies, causes, or likely outcomes
evaluate — determine what is significant, risky, useful, or action-relevant
