# Conflict And Emotional Routing v0

## Purpose

This document locks emotional routing and conflict handling in the current
`v.03` canon.

It defines the role of emotional expression flags, the kernel's routing
exception, the canonical transition ladder, the conflict being, and the
differentiator trigger.

## Strain

`strain` is pre-failure load. It is present when a being is still holding its current interpretation coherently, but holding that coherence is getting expensive. It comes before explicit mismatch — earlier than conflict.

`strain` is self-reported by the being paying the cost. It is not stamped by the kernel. It is not inferred by another being.

`strain` is private by default. A being may experience it without expressing it outward.

When a being chooses to surface `strain` outward, it becomes `stress` or `anger` on expression — not `strain`. That surfacing is chosen disclosure, not mandatory telemetry.

The hippocampus listens for `strain`. Its role is to reinstate fit before failure when possible while the load is still internal.

When a being surfaces `strain` as `stress` or `anger`, it is making itself vulnerable — saying it is still holding but holding is costly. That may later become retained experience and trust may move through that retained consequence.

## Emotional Signals

`strain` is not a being.

It is internal pre-failure load inside the being's present.

It does not travel outward as its own expression field.

`stress` and `anger` are not beings.

They are optional scalar flags carried on expression.

Each uses a `1` to `100` spectrum.

They do not belong to inference.

They do not belong to cognition.

They are structural values on expression that the kernel reads at every hop.

When a being chooses to surface internal `strain` outward, it does so as
`stress` or `anger` on expression.

In the current canon, `stress` is the surfaced reach signal.

It may set `trace_token` TTL for relationship-strength accumulation.

`anger` is the surfaced escalation signal.

The kernel is the only component that acts on them structurally.

## Kernel Emotional Routing

The kernel owns emotional-signal routing as a class of behavior.

This is the narrow exception to the kernel otherwise remaining a dumb router.

At every hop, the kernel reads emotional values on expression and applies fixed
routing rules.

If an expression carries `anger` above the defined threshold, the kernel routes
a copy of that expression to the prefrontal being.

The original expression continues walking its normal path unchanged.

The kernel does not infer.

It does not judge.

It reads a value and applies one rule.

More emotional signal types may be added as they are encountered.

## Transition Points

`strain` becomes outward `stress` or `anger` when a being chooses to surface
its internal holding-cost in expression.

That transition is disclosure.

It is not kernel inference.

It is not another being naming the load from outside.

A mismatch becomes explicit `conflict` when the prefrontal judges that repair
did not restore fit and closes the failing exchange.

At that point the break is no longer latent load or routed escalation.

It is explicit post-break `conflict`.

## Conflict Resolution Ladder

The canonical ladder is:

1. A being carries `strain` internally.
2. When that load surfaces outward, it becomes `stress` or `anger` on the
   expression.
3. The kernel reads `anger` at every hop. Above threshold, it routes a copy to
   the prefrontal while the original expression continues unchanged.
4. The prefrontal surfaces mismatch, attempts repair, and proposes a new path.
5. If repair fails, the prefrontal closes the failing exchange. That is where
   the mismatch becomes explicit `conflict`.
6. If the same failure repeats over time, the differentiator fires and the
   being restructures.

The system is optimistic by default.

It tries repair before reorganization.

## Conflict Being

The conflict being is not a standing monitor.

It is quiet by default.

It engages only when mismatch arrives.

Its job is narrow:

- detect deviation
- escalate to the prefrontal
- suppress the failing signal

It does not resolve conflict.

Resolution belongs to the prefrontal.

## Differentiator

The differentiator is the last resort.

It does not fire on one bad exchange.

It fires on repeated failure over time.

Its trigger is pattern, not incident.

The system keeps trying until optimism has exhausted itself.

Only then does the differentiator restructure the being.

## Conflict Versus Differentiation

The system is optimistic by default. Its default arc is: mismatch is surfaced, a new path is proposed, repair is tried before reorganization.

Two distinct problems exist:

**Language problem** — the relationship is worth keeping. The prefrontal suggests a new path. The failing exchange may still be closed if repair does not resolve it.

**Being-identity problem** — the relationship is breaking because the beings themselves are misaligned. The boundary was wrong. Repeated failure over time triggers restructuring through the differentiator.

The differentiator fires only on repeated failure over time. Its trigger is pattern, not incident. The system keeps trying until optimism has exhausted itself.

Because both prefrontal and differentiator are beings, they accumulate retained experience about failing patterns and bad fits over time. They learn what clean looks like by experiencing what dirty looks like.

## Short Framing

`strain` is internal to the being.

When it surfaces outward, it becomes `stress` or `anger` on expression.

The kernel reads emotional flags structurally.

Above threshold, the kernel routes a copy to the prefrontal while the original
expression keeps walking.

The prefrontal attempts repair and closes the exchange if repair fails.

That closure is the transition into explicit `conflict`.

The conflict being detects deviation and suppresses the failing signal.

The prefrontal owns resolution.

The differentiator fires only when failure repeats over time.
