# Relationship Worlds

Every peer relationship grows its own world.

A being's peer is not just an exchange stack holding conversation history. It is a world of beings that grew out of that relationship. The exchange stack is the conversation history. The world is what got built from it.

Two beings relate. Through that relating, new beings emerge — concepts, skills, shared understanding. Those live in a world that belongs to that relationship. Not a flat stack of impulses. A populated space.

This is differentiation at the relationship level, not just at the being level. Each being has their own world per peer. The kernel sees all worlds. Each relationship grows its own.

## Implementation

World beings (see `skyra v.03 changes/world-beings.md`) are the concrete form of this. Skyra grows a world being for a relationship. All beings that emerge from that relationship live inside it.

## What This Changes

In v.04, a peer carries an exchange map and a history. In a relationship-world model, the peer carries a full world — a set of beings that exist because of the relationship. Those beings are not global. They belong to the relationship that produced them.

## Open Questions
