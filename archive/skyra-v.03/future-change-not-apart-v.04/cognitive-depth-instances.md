# Cognitive Depth Instances

A being can run as two instances at different cognitive depths — one fast, one deep — and be in relationship with itself.

The fast instance handles reactive work. When it hits a decision point that needs deeper synthesis — for example, prefrontal deciding whether it has enough to act toward the external world — it fires at the deep instance of itself. Same identity. Same purpose. Same memory. Different inference runner underneath.

The deep instance reasons slowly, returns a judgment. The fast instance acts on it.

## Why this is not two beings

They share memory. The hippocampus and experience store are the same being's. Retained experience formed by the deep instance is available to the fast instance and vice versa. Ontologically they are one being running at two cognitive depths.

## The relationship

The fast and deep instances are in relationship with each other. The fast instance consults the deep instance the same way prefrontal consults strategy — fires an expression, waits for a return, acts on the result. The relational debt model applies. The deep instance owes something back.

## Genome expression

The runner could be declared per instance in the genome:

```
skyra being ~name prefrontal-fast ~runner qwen2.5 ...
skyra being ~name prefrontal-deep ~runner deepseek-r1 ...
```

Or it could be a property of the instance type rather than a named being — the blueprint spawns both at boot and wires them together.

## Open questions

- Does the deep instance have its own exchange stack with the fast instance or do they share one?
- Can any being have a depth instance or is this specific to prefrontal?
- What is the callable language on the fast → deep relationship — what kinds of questions warrant deep reasoning?
