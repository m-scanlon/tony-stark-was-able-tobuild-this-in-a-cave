# Sense Being

The sense being is the full boundary between the runtime and the world. It owns both directions — inbound and outbound. It is the single point where the runtime meets everything external.

It is cognitive. It reasons through inference.

## What It Does

**Inbound** — receives raw natural language from external beings. Michael just talks. The sense being interprets what he wants, understands what beings exist and what they can do, and translates that into valid protocol syntax before anything enters the kernel. The runtime never sees raw input. Everything downstream sees valid protocol.

**Outbound** — receives protocol from the runtime and translates it back into whatever form the external being expects. Natural language back to Michael. An API call to an external service. A process invocation. The sense being owns that translation.

The sense being is not a relay. Not a filter. It is a translator with judgment. It must be faithful to what Michael said — not interpret, translate. The intent must survive the translation intact.

## Why Cognitive

The sense being has to understand what Michael wants. That requires inference. It also has to know what beings exist in the runtime and what they can do — so it can form valid syntax without hallucinating beings or expressions that don't exist. That requires a present that includes the runtime's current topology.

## Singleton At The Boundary

One sense being. It owns all external exchanges — one per external contact. When Michael talks to Skyra, the sense being owns that exchange. When an email arrives, the sense being owns that exchange too. Separate exchanges, one being. That is what gives it cross-thread context — it holds everything happening at the boundary simultaneously.

## Thread Origin

Every thread originates at the sense being. An external signal arrives. The sense being interprets it, forms valid protocol, and passes it inward with a threadID. That threadID is the thread. Everything downstream serves it.

## Relationship To The Old Sensory Being

Sensory was a fixed relay — receive and route inward without interpretation. The sense being replaces it. The sense being is what sensory was trying to be.

## Relationship To Execution Surface

The sense being's outbound execution surface is the process layer — CLI, HTTP, voice, whatever Michael's devices speak. The sense being declares its outbound surfaces in its callable language. New surfaces are added through the genome. No hardcoding beyond the process primitive.

## Open Questions

- How does the sense being's present render the current runtime topology — what beings exist, what their callable language is — without becoming overwhelming?
- Can the sense being be wrong — mistranslate Michael's intent? What is the correction path?
- Does the sense being seed new relationships when a new external contact arrives, or does that happen downstream?
- How does the sense being handle simultaneous inbound signals from multiple of Michael's surfaces — does it merge them into one thread or treat them independently?
- Does the name "sense" still fit now that it owns output as well as input?
