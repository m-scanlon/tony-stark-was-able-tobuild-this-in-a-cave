A separate mechanism — a dedicated write call, a structured form — is good for deliberate moments. You sit down to record, you fill the fields, you submit. Clean. Inline self-report — what you just did, embedded in the flow of an exchange — is lower friction and more honest. You're recording while you're in it, not reconstructing afterward. **Structured write** — explicit call with fields populated. Used when you're deliberately logging.

**Inline capture** — a marked block in an exchange (something like what you just did) that gets ingested as a raw moment and either auto-structured or flagged for light cleanup. The exchange_ref fills itself in automatically from context.
